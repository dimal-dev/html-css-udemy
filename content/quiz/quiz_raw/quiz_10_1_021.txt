  </Description>
  <Answer 1>
    <Is Correct>Yes</Is Correct>

    <Explanation>Why? CSS shorthand values for padding follow a clockwise order starting from the top.</Explanation>
  </Answer 1>
  <Answer 2>
    <Is Correct>No</Is Correct>
    <Content>Left, Right, Top, Bottom</Content>
    <Explanation>Why? That order does not follow the clockwise rule used in CSS.</Explanation>
  </Answer 2>
  <Answer 3>
    <Is Correct>No</Is Correct>
    <Content>Top, Left, Bottom, Right</Content>
    <Explanation>Why? This order skips the clockwise rule and would confuse the browser.</Explanation>

<Question 7>
  <Description>
    What happens when you set padding: 10px 20px;?
  </Description>
  <Answer 1>
    <Is Correct>No</Is Correct>
    <Content>Top and bottom get 20px, left and right get 10px</Content>
    <Explanation>Why? That's incorrect order. First is top/bottom, second is left/right.</Explanation>
  </Answer 1>
  <Answer 2>
    <Is Correct>Yes</Is Correct>
    <Content>Top and bottom: 10px; Left and right: 20px</Content>
    <Explanation>Why? Two-value padding syntax applies first to vertical, second to horizontal sides.</Explanation>
  </Answer 2>
  <Answer 3>
    <Is Correct>No</Is Correct>
    <Content>All sides get 15px</Content>
    <Explanation>Why? Only one-value syntax applies equally to all sides, not two-value syntax.</Explanation>
  </Answer 3>
